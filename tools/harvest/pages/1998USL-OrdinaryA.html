NOTES:

  • Dates in the first column are for the current liturgical year, with links to the readings provided on the website of the USCCB, if available.
  • Numbers in the second column are the official reading numbers from the Ordo Lectionum Missae, second typical edition, 1981. Each of these is one less than the number for the corresponding readings in the first typical edition of the Lectionary, from 1969, since the Chrism Mass (on Thursday of Holy Week) was moved from #39 in the 1969 edition to #260 in the 1981 edition.
  • Titles in the third column pertain to most years; in each year, however, some Sundays of Ordinary Time are supplanted by special Solemnities and Feast Days.
  • Readings that are new or different from the 1970 Lectionary are marked in the table above; click on the underlined links (new) or (diff) for detailed explanations.
  • Printing Suggestion: To print this table more clearly, try using "LANDSCAPE" rather than "Portrait" as the paper orientation.
  • In 2026, the 7th, 8th, 9th, and 10th Sundays of Ordinary Time are omitted, due to the moveable dates of Easter and of the Solemnities of the Lord.
  • The Third Sunday of Ordinary Time is now celebrated as the "Sunday of the Word of God," as decreed in the Apostolic Letter, Aperuit Illis, by Pope Francis (Sept. 30, 2019).
  • A Retreat with the Gospel of Matthew: An audio program with twelve 25-min. retreat conferences (download MP3s) and a written guide for prayer and reflection; from Learn 25 (formerly "Now You Know Media").

RATIONALE:

For an explanation about which readings were chosen for the Sundays in Ordinary Time and how they are arranged, see the "General Introduction to the Lectionary," Chapter V, Part 5.
